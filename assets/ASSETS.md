# Kindred demo assets checklist

The v2 demo includes original local SVG placeholders so nothing breaks, but the product will feel even better if you replace them with carefully chosen real or generated art.

## Folder structure

```text
assets/
  people/
  places/
  sponsors/
  circles/
  icons/
  placeholders/
```

Keep the same filenames if you want to replace art without editing `app.js`.

---

## People photos

Recommended count: 10 square portraits.

Current files:

```text
assets/people/maya.svg

assets/people/jules.svg
assets/people/sofia.svg
assets/people/mina.svg
assets/people/ren.svg
assets/people/amelia.svg
```

Recommended replacement specs:

- Ratio: 1:1
- Minimum size: 800 × 800
- Format: `.jpg`, `.webp`, `.png`, or `.svg`
- Style: natural, warm, friendly, believable
- Diversity: age, ethnicity, body type, style, personality
- Avoid: dating-app thirst traps, hyper-polished model shots, corporate stock smiles, obviously romantic/couple photos

Preferred emotional tone:

- Cozy
- Curious
- Approachable
- Not overly posed
- Activity-adjacent when possible: book, coffee, cinema, sketchbook, park, museum


Recommended replacement specs:

- Ratio: 3:2 or 16:10
- Minimum size: 1200 × 800
- Format: `.jpg`, `.webp`, `.png`, or `.svg`
- Locations to cover:
  - Library
  - Small cinema
  - Independent bookstore
  - Warm café
  - Museum/gallery
  - Daylight park route
  - Board game café
  - Creative studio/workshop
  - Public market

Avoid:

- Isolated alleyways
- Private homes
- Empty parking lots
- Dark, unsafe-looking night exteriors
- Club/nightlife visuals that make the app feel hookup-coded

## Copy guidance for future assets

Every image should support the product thesis:

> Safe public plans with people who make the activity better.

When choosing visuals, ask:

1. Does this look like a place someone would safely meet a new person?
2. Does it feel activity-first instead of attraction-first?
3. Does it reduce social anxiety?
4. Does it make the user think, “I would actually go to this”?
